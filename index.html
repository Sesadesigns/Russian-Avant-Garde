Russian Avant Garde Library

Between 1890 and 1930, a group of radical artists in the Russian Empire and early Soviet Union set out to reinvent the visual world from scratch. They called into question everything — form, colour, space, and the very purpose of art itself — and in doing so produced one of the most electrifying movements in modern history. From Kazimir Malevich to Alexander Rodchenko, from Natalia Goncharova and to El Lissitzky, the Russian Avant-Garde was never just an aesthetic revolution. It was a philosophical one — a belief that art could reshape consciousness, remake society, and build a new human future. This library is a compilation of the artists who gave them form. Browse the collection, filter by disciplines, trace the movements — Futurism, Suprematism, Constructivism, Rayonism, Cubo-Futurism, Theatre, Abstract, and Rayonism — and discover biographies of the geniuses behind.
